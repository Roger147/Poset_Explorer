## Concepts Clarified

- The zeta function is a structural comparability indicator on a poset.
- A zeta transform acts on values assigned to poset elements by accumulating
  them over downsets.
- The Mobius function supplies inverse coefficients for undoing zeta-style
  cumulative sums.
- Mobius inversion is the recovery operation, while Mobius values are the
  structural coefficients used by that operation.
- Interval statistics study bounded local subposets `[x, y]` without storing
  every interval explicitly.
- Histograms are compact relative to full matrices, though Mobius-value
  histograms are not guaranteed to have a fixed number of keys.
- Boolean lattice elements can be represented as subset labels while the
  factory still emits ordinary `Poset` instances.
